WEEK 3

User alignment:

The goal for my website is to make it as user friendly as possible. I aim to make it easy to navigate and understand for the user, so they are able to view my pages such as my portfolio work without having to click onto countless links. When users enter my website the goal is to design it in such a way that they are not overwhelmed by what they see so that they are to digest what is in front of them and be able to view whatever they may be looking for using the 3-Click Rule. As for who the target audience of my website will be, as for specifics, anyone who can use a piece of technology to access my site. Hence my desire to make it as user friendly as possible, having a catchy yet not too overwhelming layout that will encourage people to look at my work.

Reflection on structure and URL scheme:

Based on what I have mentioned so far, my sites navigation structure will depend on the filing structure that I had drawn up in previous discussions. That means ensuring I give all my sites content the appropriate naming conventions as well as there being buttons or a navigation bar that can be accessed on any page of my website, should the user need to navigate somewhere else quickly on my page. My URL scheme will reflect this navigation by making the user aware what paths they are currently on on my website. I believe this will help keep things consistent and usable in the long run. In relation to semantic markup, in order for my site to communicate clearly with my target audience I need to use the appropriate HTML tags that will make it easy for myself to structure my website how I want as well as maintaining consistency with my audience.
