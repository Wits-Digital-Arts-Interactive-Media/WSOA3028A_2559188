WEEK 2

Folder structure and URL scheme:

My plan is to have a simple and concise folder structure. Each folder will contain components I would need for my website such as images, links to other sites, my blog and essay posts. As I continue building my website, I plan on refining this structure and changing it as I go. In terms of my URL scheme, I plan on making a user friendly, semantic URL scheme that it is easily readable to users and one that informs the user where they are and what paths are accessible to them on my website. I am still undecided on whether to make it a local (.co.za) OR international domain (.com). Here are a few examples I made (note none of them lead anywhere, hopefully):

Wireframes

What I am aiming at in my wireframes is a user friendly design for PC and cellphone users. I did not want to make a single page to complicated on any device, each page will have a similar design, an image associated with the page, the page content, a crossbar or index image leading to different pages on the website and the title of the page in a readable and alluring font. Reading Reflection:

What I understood from the reading, based on the relation of hypertext and McLuhan's Laws of Media, is that the internet has been a place where ideas and concepts have been recycled, rediscovered and reinvented. Rarely has there been something revolutionary added to it. The internet today relies on hypertext, interconnected nodes of information which we as users can interact with. The way we interact with that information is different to the linear structure of reading information from books, allowing us to interact with things all at once instead of one at a time. Hypertext enhances how we interact with the internet by presenting us with multiple streams of information which can be accessed at anytime while making more conventional modes of linear media obsolete due to how accessible everything is now. With it being able to access everything, older concepts similar to Memex or Xanadu are easily retrievable and can be augmented into the internet, pushing it to its limits and creating new modes of expression and understanding.

What does interaction mean to me?

What interaction means to me is that for something to be interactable it must first be made human-friendly, meaning a random person will be able to learn and be able to use something new with ease and understanding. In the context of the WWW, a website or page should be easy to navigate and not confuse the user of the site. As a designer, whatever I make must be human-centered, and not difficult to understand.
